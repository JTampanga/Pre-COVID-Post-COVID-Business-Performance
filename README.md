# Pre-COVID-Post-COVID-Business-Performance
This project investigates how the COVID-19 pandemic influenced parcel delivery demand and revenue across different customer segments at ABC Company. Using Python, the dataset was cleaned, validated, and analyzed to uncover key trends before and during the pandemic period.

The analysis began with data loading and quality checks to identify missing values, verify data types, and confirm dataset structure. Descriptive statistics were then generated to understand baseline parcel volumes and year-over-year growth patterns. Customers were classified into four business segments—Enterprise, Large, Medium, and Small—based on their 2019 annual parcel volumes and corresponding contract discounts.

To evaluate pandemic impact, an Industry Standard Growth Rate (ISGR) was calculated using pre-COVID weekly volumes. The COVID observation period was defined beginning Week 12 of 2020, aligning with lockdown measures. Weekly parcel volumes were compared across years to identify demand spikes and shifts in customer behavior. Additional analysis reclassified customers using 2020 volumes to assess how pandemic demand caused customers to move into higher volume tiers.

Visualizations were developed to illustrate volume growth patterns, customer transitions, and seasonal peaks. The results highlighted significant increases in parcel demand during lockdown periods, particularly among enterprise and large customers, providing insight into operational scaling needs and revenue opportunities.
